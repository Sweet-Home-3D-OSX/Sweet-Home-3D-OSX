## Overview

Home design decisions — furniture purchases, paint colours, room layouts, renovation plans — are difficult to make confidently from imagination alone. <a href="#">Sweet Home 3D</a> provides the visual feedback that makes these decisions easier, allowing any Mac user to build a precise digital model of a room or home and see proposed changes in three dimensions before spending money or beginning physical work.

The dual-view interface keeps the <a href="#">2D plan</a> and the 3D visualisation visible simultaneously, so every change made in the top-down floor plan view is immediately visible in the three-dimensional perspective below. This real-time connection between plan and view makes design exploration fast — moving a sofa in the floor plan shows the spatial impact of the change in 3D immediately, without any rendering delay or view refresh required. The <a href="#">compass</a> and sun position tools allow the model to be oriented relative to real-world north, enabling accurate sunlight simulation that shows how natural light will enter the space at different times of day and year — a genuinely useful planning tool for decisions about window treatments, furniture placement relative to light sources, and room orientation.

Importing <a href="#">custom 3D models</a> in OBJ and DAE formats allows specific furniture pieces, fixtures, or architectural elements that are not in the built-in library to be added to the design. Manufacturers and furniture retailers publish 3D models of their products that can be imported directly, enabling the design to reflect specific real products rather than generic stand-ins. The <a href="#">texture library</a> covers wall finishes, flooring materials, and fabric upholstery options that can be applied to any surface in the model, allowing paint colours, tile patterns, and material choices to be evaluated visually before purchase. Export options include <a href="#">OBJ</a> for use in external rendering software, SVG and PDF for floor plan output, and high-resolution image export from the rendered 3D view — giving Sweet Home 3D designs a range of useful output formats for sharing, printing, and further professional use beyond the application itself.

- <a href="#">Sun simulation</a> with compass orientation for daylighting analysis

## Additional Information

The ability to import manufacturer 3D models into Sweet Home 3D transforms the furniture placement stage from generic arrangement to specific product planning. Placing the exact sofa, kitchen unit, or bathroom fixture under consideration in the model and seeing it in context with accurate dimensions provides a level of purchase confidence that showroom visits alone cannot match.

Sun simulation with compass orientation adds a layer of practical environmental analysis that makes Sweet Home 3D useful for decisions beyond aesthetics. Understanding how natural light will behave in a space throughout the day and across seasons is relevant to furniture placement, window treatment choices, and the assessment of a potential renovation or purchase.

Sweet Home 3D's export capabilities extend its usefulness beyond the planning stage. Floor plan PDFs provide contractors with dimensioned reference documents; rendered images communicate design intent to collaborators and family members; OBJ export allows the model to be taken into professional rendering software for higher-quality output when needed.
